Process how i work.

Circular by design. Not a linear double diamond – a systemic framework that moves through phases fluidly, loops back intentionally, and treats context as part of the solution. AI accelerates every stage without replacing the human at the centre.

My take on AI-augmented design: I use AI as a genuine capability multiplier – execution speed, prototype validation, reiteration capacity. This enables a non-linear process where design and development build together, not in sequence. With Figma MCPs, Claude Code, and custom skills: the strategic inputter mind shapes what AI produces.

Where I focus most: Increasingly on the strategic side – product direction, system architecture, outcome framing. AI handles repetitive screen patterns, freeing me for what matters: supporting the product's own evolution. I use AI to validate early, not add frills late.

01

Explore

Diverge wide into the problem space. User research, stakeholder interviews, data analysis, systems mapping. Developers and product join early to surface constraints. AI accelerates synthesis – affinity clustering, research summarisation, pattern detection – so depth isn't sacrificed for speed.

02

Reframe

Shift perspective before converging. Challenge assumptions, reframe the problem from multiple vantage points, identify leverage points within the system. This is where Orientation & Vision Setting becomes critical – aligning on the right direction before committing to solutions.

03

Create

Generate, prototype, test. AI-assisted UI generation, rapid prototyping, early engineer co-creation. Sample users join for quick-loop concept testing. Connections & Relationships inform how solutions interact with the broader ecosystem – not just the screen in front of you.

04

Catalyse

Ship, measure, and feed back in. This is not the end – it's the return point. Real usage data reshapes the next cycle. Leadership & Storytelling sustains momentum; Continuing the Journey keeps the system evolving. Circular, not closed.

No one-size-fits-all. Every project gets a tailored blend of design thinking, systems thinking, Fitts's/Hick's/Jakob's laws, and domain-specific knowledge. The phases above are a compass with loops – not a linear prescription.

FAQ dive in.

I've compiled some of the most common questions to help you understand how I function.

01. What's your background?+
I bring 10+ years across UX/UI, strategy, research, branding, content, and marketing. That mix gives me an edge: I don't just design screens – I shape how a product communicates, converts, and earns trust, end to end.
02. What's your design philosophy?+
User needs must be matched. Less is more. And complexity needs to be untangled. In nowadays market, features don't differentiate one player from another, be it a SaaS, app, or website. It's rather the quality of the interaction that users experience through the services offered and the lack of friction points. That is the real differentiator to me. The product that wins is the one that helps people get to the outcome faster, easier, and with more confidence. I design to remove friction with high craft, clear systems, and decisions grounded in evidence.
03. What's your "superpower"?+
I'm a zoom-lens designer: macro strategy, micro precision. I'm detail-obsessed by nature – the kind of person who notices what others miss – and I can zoom out just as fast to shape the end-to-end flow, narrative, and product direction. That's how experiences feel premium and work brilliantly.
04. What does your usual process look like?+
My process follows the spirit of double diamond approach and design thinking principles: explore the problem widely, define what matters, then iterate toward the best solution that delivers results. When the journey spans touchpoints beyond the UI, I map the wider system (often via a lightweight service blueprint) to spot gaps, dependencies, and moments that make or break trust. Then I run fast loops of research, prototyping, and testing – aligning the team on outcomes, shipping in slices, and improving post-launch based on real usage.
05. How do you handle trade-offs?+
I like to reframe problems as a moving piece in constant evolution. Treating roadmaps as dynamic tools to redefine due to changes in budget, time, or needs, helps me to be ready for disruptions – because they do happen. I stay calm under ambiguity, and I keep momentum by breaking work into testable slices instead of big bets.
06. How do you approach AI in design?+
I myself use and am a big embracer of AI-augmented design. It adds above all, execution speed, prototype validation, and reiteration capabilities. It allows for a non-linear process, where design and development build the magic together, not just pass the ball to the other.

Having 3 different Figma MCP (1 official, 2 not) in place, playing around with Claude Code, and testing absurd numbers of SKILL.md(s), I can't express anything but mind-blown propensity for AI. As per now, we are in the realm of "output generative AI", so let's be the strategic input behind it.

With it, I anticipate needs, foresee roadmap blockers, and use it as a sounding board to help me read and adapt to ever-evolving usage data, shifting from static user flows to adaptable modular systems.
07. What's a complex design challenge you've navigated?+
At Swarco, I led product design for MyCity, a complex mobility management and traffic-engineering SaaS platform used by major cities like London, Paris, Los Angeles, and Doha. I aligned PMs and engineering on clear outcomes, then ran iterative municipality research + usability tests. I redesigned the key flows through rapid prototyping and validation, and scaled a component-based design system. UX maturity increased, resulting in: +25% delivery velocity, –30% design debt/handoff time, and +20% user satisfaction.

My Story

Warm Italy

I was born in warm Italy (although it was the middle of winter). Where I absorbed and developed my senses, quite literally. My strongest childhood memories are painted with colourful citrus fruits, the best gelato ever (really), inundating basil notes, but also millennial history, exemplary architecture, naturally perfect landscapes, warm salty water and never-ending sunshine.

Shortly after high school, I realised that doing an Erasmus exchange would have been my launching platform to better english, international friendships and mind-opening experiences, all with lots of travel around Europe. Yes, it represents the key that turned me, young Italian boy, into a European adult hungry for more of this world.

pack n' go

I studied at the Polytechnic University of Milan and earned a Master's in Brand Communication (designing, building, and managing brands). I loved the strategic side – the storytelling, the positioning, the clarity – but after a few professional experiences, I felt something missing. I needed a break from forming my professional self, and to work on myself, so I started backpacking across South America. I still remember the feeling of insignificance in that majestic nature, while at the same time feeling my shoulders growing, becoming wider, and my ideas clearer.

My Beloved ABbC

I couldn't go back home anymore, "abroad" was my new home. But where, exactly? I drafted a few ideas, pros and cons, and suddenly I had shortlisted on my unmissable Moleskin the final 4 cities that were inspiring, intriguing and calling me the most: Amsterdam, Barcelona, Berlin, and Copenhagen. And without realizing it, I ended up threading my life through all four of those amazing cities.

From lots of long weekends cycling and wandering in Amsterdam; to winter-escapes in the warm and colourful Barcelona; until getting married to my beloved inspiring wife in one of the best designed cities I've ever experienced – Copenhagen.

Berlin, Du weißt ich liebe dich so viel

Berlin didn't welcome me with white gloves or shortcuts. It welcomed me with reality. Prenzlauer Berg, Kreuzberg, Neukölln – full of memories, fun, learnings, drama, growth, friends and joy. Berlin welcomed me as a young adult and led me to the next life chapter as a better designer, and eventually as the Father of a true Berliner.

And Now? Oh Canada…

Now, I say Bonjour Montréal, Alicia's birthplace, my very much beloved and inspiring wife, and my newest chapter. There is lots to adjust to, learn (i.e. de-rusting my 5th language, French), discover, explore, and enjoy. My feedback? It's too early for a definitive opinion but I already know this: I can breathe here and I have all the nature I need to enjoy and feel happy.

Weekends are alive with outdoor sports from snowboarding to cross-country skiing to mountain biking and kitesurfing. And when I need a slower tempo, you'll find me snowshoeing with a camera.

Design as a Fil-Rouge

Design is my fil-rouge: it's the outstanding architecture I've observed that helps me create scalable systems and uncomplicated platforms; it's the nuances of colours and opinions that taught me the power of human perception and how to guide users through my apps; it's the nature I've immersed in during my travels that gives me a special perspective and sense of aesthetics.

I've spent the last 10+ years working across UX/UI, product strategy, content, and brand – because great products aren't just usable. They're coherent. They're engaging. They're solving real life problems. They earn trust. They move the business forward without disrespecting the people using them.

That's why what I'm most drawn to is simple: making complex things feel easy. High craft, no friction. Systems that scale. Proof over opinions. And a team environment where we ship great work without losing our humanity, lightness, and fun doing it.

If any of that sounds like the kind of designer you want in the room, I'd love to talk.
